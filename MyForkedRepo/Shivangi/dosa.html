Masala Dosa

A South Indian specialty consisting of a thin crispy crepe filled with spiced potato filling.

Ingredients

  • For Dosa Batter:
  • 2 cups rice (preferably parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup poha (flattened rice)
  • 1 tablespoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • For Potato Filling:
  • 4 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • For Serving:
  • Coconut chutney
  • Sambar
  • Ghee or oil for cooking dosas

Directions

  1. For Dosa Batter:
  2. Wash and soak rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 4-5 hours.
  3. Soak poha for 30 minutes before grinding.
  4. Drain the water from all the soaked ingredients.
  5. First, grind urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds to a smooth paste using water as needed.
  6. Then grind rice and poha to a smooth paste.
  7. Mix both the pastes together in a large container. Add salt and mix well.
  8. Cover and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours or overnight.
  9. The batter should rise and become slightly frothy when fermented properly.
  10. For Potato Filling:
  11. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  12. Add cumin seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds.
  13. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  14. Add grated ginger and sauté for a minute.
  15. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder (if using), and salt. Mix well.
  16. Add mashed potatoes and mix thoroughly. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  17. Add chopped coriander leaves and lemon juice. Mix well and turn off the heat.
  18. To Make Dosa:
  19. Heat a non-stick or cast iron pan/tawa on medium heat.
  20. Once hot, sprinkle a few drops of water. If they sizzle immediately, the pan is ready.
  21. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the pan.
  22. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  23. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and on top of the dosa.
  24. Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp.
  25. Place a spoonful of potato filling in the center of the dosa.
  26. Fold the dosa in half or roll it to enclose the filling.
  27. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
