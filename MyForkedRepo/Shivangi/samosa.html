Samosa

A popular fried or baked pastry snack, originating from the Middle East and now a staple in South Asian cuisine, typically filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and other vegetables.

Ingredients

  • For Dough:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water as needed
  • For Filling:
  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • For Frying:
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For Serving:
  • Mint chutney
  • Tamarind chutney

Directions

  1. For Dough:
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, carom seeds, and salt.
  3. Add ghee or oil and rub it into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Gradually add water and knead to form a firm dough.
  5. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  6. For Filling:
  7. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  8. Add ginger and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
  9. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  10. Add mashed potatoes and green peas. Mix thoroughly.
  11. Add garam masala and dried mango powder. Mix well.
  12. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  13. Add chopped coriander leaves and mix. Turn off the heat and let the filling cool.
  14. To Assemble and Fry:
  15. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  16. Roll each ball into an oval or circle of about 6-7 inches in diameter.
  17. Cut the rolled dough in half to form two semicircles.
  18. Take one semicircle and moisten the straight edge with water.
  19. Fold it to form a cone, sealing the moistened edge.
  20. Fill the cone with the potato filling, leaving the top edge empty.
  21. Moisten the top edge with water and press to seal, forming a triangular shape.
  22. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  23. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  24. Fry the samosas on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crisp.
  25. Drain on paper towels.
  26. To Serve:
  27. Serve hot with mint chutney and tamarind chutney.
