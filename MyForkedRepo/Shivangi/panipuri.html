Pani Puri

A popular Indian street food consisting of hollow, crispy-fried balls filled with a mixture of potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and spices, then dipped in flavored water.

Ingredients

  • For Puris:
  • 2 cups semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For Potato Filling:
  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • For Mint-Coriander Water (Green Pani):
  • 1 cup mint leaves
  • 1 cup coriander leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1 inch piece of ginger
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind pulp
  • 1 tablespoon chaat masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon black salt
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 cups cold water
  • For Sweet Tamarind Chutney:
  • 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1/4 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste

Directions

  1. For Puris:
  2. Mix semolina and all-purpose flour in a bowl. Add water gradually to make a stiff dough.
  3. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into thin circles.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the puris until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  6. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
  7. For Potato Filling:
  8. Mix mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, chaat masala, cumin powder, salt, and coriander leaves in a bowl.
  9. Set aside.
  10. For Mint-Coriander Water:
  11. Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, and ginger with a little water to make a smooth paste.
  12. Add tamarind pulp, chaat masala, cumin powder, black salt, and salt. Mix well.
  13. Add cold water and adjust the seasoning. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
  14. For Sweet Tamarind Chutney:
  15. Cook tamarind pulp with jaggery or sugar until it thickens.
  16. Add cumin powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and let it cool.
  17. To Assemble:
  18. Make a small hole in the center of each puri.
  19. Fill with potato mixture.
  20. Dip in the mint-coriander water and serve immediately with a drizzle of sweet tamarind chutney.
