Pav Bhaji

A popular street food from Maharashtra consisting of a spicy vegetable mash served with soft bread rolls.

Ingredients

    For Bhaji

  • 2 boiled potatoes
  • ½ cup peas, ½ cup capsicum, 1 chopped onion, 1 chopped tomato
  • 1 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 medium capsicum (bell pepper), finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 2-3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • Coriander leaves, finely chopped for garnish
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 1 onion, finely chopped for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat butter in a large pan. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes, capsicum, and green peas. Cook until the vegetables are soft.
  4. Add the mashed potatoes, pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add about 1 cup of water and mash the vegetables with a potato masher while cooking.
  6. Cook the bhaji for about 15-20 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally. Add more water if needed to maintain a thick consistency.
  7. Meanwhile, slice the pav buns horizontally, but not all the way through.
  8. Heat a tava or flat pan, add some butter, and toast the pav buns until golden brown on both sides.
  9. Serve the hot bhaji garnished with chopped coriander leaves, chopped onions, and a squeeze of lemon juice, alongside the buttered pav buns.
