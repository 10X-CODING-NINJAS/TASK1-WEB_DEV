Chole Bhature

A popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpeas (chole) served with deep-fried bread (bhatura).

Ingredients

  • For Chole (Chickpea Curry):
  • 2 cups chickpeas (soaked overnight and boiled)
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 teaspoons chole masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 tea bag (for color)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Warm water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Directions

  1. For Chole:
  2. Soak chickpeas overnight, then pressure cook with a tea bag, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom until soft. Discard the tea bag and keep the spices.
  3. Heat oil in a large pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add chole masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add boiled chickpeas along with some of the cooking water. Mix well.
  8. Mash some chickpeas with the back of a spoon to thicken the gravy.
  9. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  10. Add garam masala and mix well. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. For Bhature:
  12. In a large bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, yogurt, sugar, baking soda, oil, and salt.
  13. Gradually add warm water and knead to form a soft, smooth dough.
  14. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 2-3 hours.
  15. Divide the dough into small balls.
  16. Roll each ball into an oval or round shape.
  17. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  18. Slide the rolled bhature into the hot oil. Press gently with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
  19. Fry until golden brown on both sides.
  20. Drain on paper towels.
  21. To Serve:
  22. Serve hot chole with bhature, along with sliced onions and lemon wedges on the side.
